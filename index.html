Our Saga: A Tale of Fire, Water, and Forever

In the age of modern wanderers, beneath the southern stars and scent of flame - kissed meat,
two kindred spirits crossed paths at a great gathering — a friend’s braai, where the mead flowed and tales were shared.
It was there that Alec, a bold-hearted warrior with a grin like sunrise, first laid eyes upon Tanita, a shieldmaiden whose spirit burned brighter than the hearth.

Their first quest began not with sword or storm, but with laughter and a round of adventure golf.
Yet as all great tales go, their path was not without peril — for as they journeyed to a stand of fine meats run by Tanita's kin,
they were halted by the city guard. A test of patience and valor, which they passed with heads held high and stomachs rumbling for wors.

Only moons later, Alec sought to bind their fates forevermore.
Beneath the roar of falling waters — an element Tanita had never beheld with her own eyes — he knelt and offered his heart.
She accepted with joy... then charged toward the cascading wonder, leaving him in stunned silence.
It was the swiftest "yes" and sprint in Viking betrothal lore.
From that moment, they were as one.

Within a week, Alec left his great hall vacant and joined Tanita, her brother and cousin in a new home,
forging a clan bound not by blood, but by love and mischief.
Their bond, tested and tempered by time, carried them across seas and strange lands — immigrants to a new world, adventurers through and through.

They slept in castles and curled in carriages barely fit for a child, let alone a man of Alec’s size.
They woke on mountain edges, breath stolen by the sight of vast gorges stretching like Fenrir’s yawning jaws.
Their travels were not planned — they were conjured by fate, and the gods always seemed to smile upon them.

This union, though twice celebrated, will now be sealed as true and eternal.
Their first wedding — cloaked in purple magic at a realm akin to Africa Burn — was wild and wondrous.
But now, with kin as witness and the old gods invoked, they bind themselves in sacred vow.

Together with their beloved small warrior — whose strength is mighty — they have weathered storm and sorrow, joy and jubilation.
Time and again they’ve risen, unbroken, proving that the three of them are forged of fire, stone, and spirit.

This is no ordinary tale.
This is a saga.
And like all great sagas, it ends not in farewell,
but in Forever Young.
